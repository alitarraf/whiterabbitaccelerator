Agreement

White Rabbit Accelerator · Cohort 1 · April 7 – July 11, 2026 · Ashland, Oregon

This agreement is between the White Rabbit Accelerator ("Program"), directed by Ali Tarraf and Thor Mueller, and the founder(s) named below ("Founder"). Please read carefully before signing.

1. Program Overview

The White Rabbit Accelerator is a 4-month cohort-based program running from April 7 to July 11, 2026 in Ashland, Oregon. The program provides mentorship, access to local resources, peer learning, and community connections to early-stage entrepreneurs in the Rogue Valley.

The program concludes with a public Demo Day on Saturday, July 11, 2026, where founders present to the community, stakeholders, and supporters.

2. Founder Commitments

By signing this agreement, the Founder commits to:

  • Weekly 1:1 Mentorship Sessions: Attend all scheduled 55-minute weekly sessions with your assigned mentor. Provide 24 hours notice if you must reschedule.
  • Monthly All-Hands Meetups: Attend all four cohort-wide meetups (approximately 2 hours each, held at end of months 1–4).
  • Demo Day: Participate fully in Demo Day on July 11, 2026, including at least one rehearsal with the mentor panel and one peer rehearsal.
  • Milestone Deliverables: Complete all program milestones on schedule, including Business Baseline (Week 2–3), SMART Goals (Week 4), MVP (Week 8), and Pitch Deck (Week 14).
  • Good Faith Participation: Engage honestly and actively with mentors, peers, and program staff. Bring real challenges. Ask for help.
  • Respect and Professionalism: Treat all cohort members, mentors, and program staff with respect. Uphold a collaborative and supportive community environment.
  • Communication: Respond to program communications within 48 hours. Use the designated program Slack channel.

3. What the Program Provides

  • Weekly 1:1 mentor sessions (55 minutes each) for 16 weeks
  • Access to Talent Makerspace, SOREDI Innovation Hub, and White Rabbit Co-working Space
  • Warm networking introductions to Rogue Valley stakeholders and business community
  • Peer cohort learning and roundtables
  • Monthly all-hands meetups hosted by the program
  • Demo Day preparation coaching and event production
  • Case-by-case access to small grant applications and support
  • Inclusion in White Rabbit Accelerator alumni network

The program does not provide equity investment, salary, or guaranteed funding outcomes.

4. Intellectual Property

The Founder retains full ownership of all intellectual property (IP) developed during the program. White Rabbit Accelerator makes no claim on the Founder's business, products, ideas, or any other IP created during or after the program.

Mentors and program staff may provide advice and feedback, but this does not create any ownership interest in the Founder's venture.

5. Confidentiality

Program participants (founders, mentors, and staff) agree to keep cohort-shared business information confidential. Founders should not share proprietary details of other founders' ventures outside the program without permission.

The Program may share anonymized outcomes (e.g. "A cohort member reached $X revenue") for promotional purposes unless the Founder opts out in writing.

6. Photography & Media

The Founder grants the White Rabbit Accelerator permission to photograph and record program events, including Demo Day, for use in promotional materials, social media, and press. The Founder may opt out of specific uses by written request.

7. Equity & Financial Terms

The White Rabbit Accelerator does not take equity in participating companies. Participation in the program is provided at no cost to accepted founders for Cohort 1. Any future fees or equity arrangements will be disclosed separately and require a new written agreement.

8. Removal from Program

The Program reserves the right to remove a Founder who consistently fails to meet commitments, engages in disrespectful behavior, or whose participation is determined to be detrimental to the cohort. Removal decisions will be made by program directors after a written warning and opportunity to remedy the issue.

9. Limitation of Liability

The White Rabbit Accelerator, its directors, mentors, and partners make no guarantees about business outcomes, funding, revenue, or success. The Founder participates voluntarily and accepts responsibility for their own business decisions.

10. Governing Law

This agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Oregon. Any disputes shall be resolved in Jackson County, Oregon.
